Uterus and Adnexa

Comment: The uterus is pear shaped, about 7‐8 cm long, and exhibits a body (fundus and isthmus) and cervix (neck). A double sheet of peritoneum (actually a mesentery) called the broad ligament envelops the ovaries, uterine tubes, and uterus. The ovarian ligament attaches the ovary to the uterus and then reflects off the uterus as the round ligament of the uterus, enters the deep inguinal ring, and ends in the labia majora as a fibrofatty mass.

The ovary is suspended laterally by the suspensory ligament of the ovary to the pelvic wall (this ligament contains the ovarian vessels, lymphatics, and autonomic nerves).

The uterine (fallopian) tubes (8‐10 cm long) are subdivided into four parts as indicated. Fertilization usually occurs in the ampulla of the uterine tube.

The broad ligament of the uterus is a double layer of peritoneum with subdivisions into the suspensory ligament of the ovary, the mesovarium, the mesosalpinx, and the mesometrium.
