Male Urethra: Sphincters

Comment: In men, only a portion of the external urethral sphincter forms a true sphincter of the urethra. It is thought that another thin portion of this muscle extends vertically to the bladder and invests the prostatic urethra anteriorly and laterally. It is unknown how or whether this muscle acts on the prostatic urethra.

At the neck of the male urinary bladder, an internal urethral sphincter (smooth muscle, sympathetically innervated from L1-L2) exists that is important during ejaculation because it prevents semen from entering the urinary bladder or urine from refluxing and entering the urinary bladder.

Clinical: Erectile dysfunction is an inability to achieve and/or maintain penile erection sufficient for sexual intercourse. Normally, sexual stimulation releases nitric oxide from nerve endings and vascular endothelial cells, thus relaxing the smooth muscle tone and increasing blood flow, which then compresses the veins draining the erectile tissues and allows for engorgement of the tissues. Drugs that treat erectile dysfunction aid in the relaxation of the smooth muscle.
