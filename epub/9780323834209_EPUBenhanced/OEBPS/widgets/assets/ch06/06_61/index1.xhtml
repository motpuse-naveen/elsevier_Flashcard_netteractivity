Cutaneous Innervation of Upper Limb: Anterior View

Comment: These cutaneous nerves reside in the subcutaneous tissue and supply the overlying skin of the upper limb. These nerves correspond to the outflow from the lower cervical plexus (clavicular region, C3-C4) and the entire brachial plexus (C5-T1). They distribute from the lateral and anterior arm and forearm (dermatomes C5-C6) to the palmar aspect of the hand (dermatomes C6-C8), and then course up the medial aspect of the forearm (C8) and arm (dermatomes C8-T1,2). These dermatome areas are approximations because they vary from person to person and often overlap, but they provide a general distribution of the cutaneous innervation.
