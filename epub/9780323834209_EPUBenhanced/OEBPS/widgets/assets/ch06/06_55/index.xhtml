Intrinsic Muscles of Hand

Origin (proximal): The three palmar interossei muscles arise from the palmar surfaces of the metacarpal bones of digits 2, 4, and 5.

Insertion (distal): Tendons of the palmar interossei insert into the extensor expansions of the digits and bases of the proximal phalanges of digits 2, 4, and 5.

Action: The palmar interossei adduct the fingers at the metacarpophalangeal joint, moving them toward an imaginary line through the axis of the middle finger (arrows). They also assist in flexing the proximal phalanx at the metacarpophalangeal joint and aid in extension of the two distal phalanges at the interphalangeal joints.

Innervation: Deep branch of the ulnar nerve (C8 and T1).

Comment: The three unipennate palmar interossei are smaller than the four dorsal interossei.

Clinical: The palmar interossei are tested clinically by asking the patient to hold a sheet of paper between two adjacent adducted fingers. As the patient holds the paper, the examiner tries to pull the paper away. With weakened adduction, the patient will have difficulty holding the sheet of paper between the fingers.
