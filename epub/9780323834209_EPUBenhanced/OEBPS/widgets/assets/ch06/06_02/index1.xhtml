Humerus and Scapula: Posterior View

X

Comment: Posteriorly, the scapula displays a prominent spine that separates the supraspinous and infraspinous fossae.

The clavicle is the first bone to ossify but the last bone to fuse and is formed by both endochondral and intramembranous ossification. It is one of the most commonly fractured bones.

Midshaft on the humerus is the deltoid tuberosity, the insertion point for the deltoid muscle.

Distally, the depression above the trochlea is called the olecranon fossa, which accommodates the olecranon of the ulna when the elbow is extended fully.

Clinical: Fractures of the scapula are relatively uncommon. Fractures of the surgical neck of the humerus are common and may injure the axillary nerve from the brachial plexus. A midshaft fracture of the humerus may injure the radial nerve.
