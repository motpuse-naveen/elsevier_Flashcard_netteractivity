Comment: The descending aorta is the direct continuation of the arch of the aorta. The descending aorta gives rise to bronchial, esophageal, pericardial, mediastinal, posterior intercostal, subcostal, and superior phrenic arteries. The descending aorta begins at the left aspect of the T4 vertebral body and descends in the posterior mediastinum to the left of the vertebral bodies. As it approaches the median plane just above the diaphragm, the aorta passes posterior to the esophagus, displacing the esophagus to the right.

Not shown in this plate but lying posterior to the esophagus is the thoracic lymphatic duct (see Card 3‐28/S4‐25).

Clinical: The small bronchial arteries (usually one right and two left arteries) are the nutritive arteries, delivering essential nutrients and oxygen to the pulmonary visceral pleura, bronchi and bronchial glands, connective tissue of the lungs, and walls of the larger intrapulmonary arteries.
