Lymphatic Drainage of Breast

Comment: The fatty tissue of the breast contains compound tubuloacinar glands (produce milk) organized into lobes, which are supported and separated from each other by connective tissue septae. The breast lies in the superficial fascia above the retromammary space, which lies above the deep pectoral fascia enveloping the pectoralis major muscle.

Most of the lymphatic drainage of the breast drains into the axillary lymph nodes with the remainder draining medially into parasternal nodes (note the direction of the arrows), or into inferior phrenic (subdiaphragmatic) nodes.

Clinical: In addition to the lymphatic spread of cancer, malignant cells may also spread via the venous system and/or direct tissue invasion.
