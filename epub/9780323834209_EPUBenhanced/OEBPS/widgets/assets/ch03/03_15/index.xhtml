Azygos Venous System

X

Comment: The azygos venous system lies on either side of the vertebral column and drains deep structures of the back, thoracic wall, and abdominal walls. Although variable in anatomy, the azygos vein arises from the inferior vena cava or right ascending lumbar vein, and the hemiazygos vein (the principal tributary of the azygos vein) arises from the left ascending lumbar vein or renal vein. Ultimately, most of the blood from the azygos system drains into the superior vena cava.

Clinical: The azygos venous system forms an important venous conduit between the inferior vena cava and the superior vena cava. The azygos veins do not contain valves, so blood may flow in different directions depending on where the pressure is highest. Via its connections with the left gastric vein in the abdomen, the azygos system is an important component of the portosystemic anastomosis in conditions such as portal hypertension.
