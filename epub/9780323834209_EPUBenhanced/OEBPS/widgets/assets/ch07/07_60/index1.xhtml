Muscles of Foot

Origin (proximal): These four bipennate muscles arise by two heads from adjacent sides of the metatarsal bones.

Insertion (distal): The 1st interosseous inserts into the medial aspect of the proximal phalanx of the second toe. The 2nd through 4th interossei insert into the lateral sides of the second to fourth toes.

Action: The dorsal interossei abduct the toes in relation to an imaginary longitudinal axis of the foot drawn through the second toe. They also flex the proximal phalanx at the metatarsophalangeal joint and extend the distal phalanges.

Innervation: Lateral plantar nerve (S2â€“S3).

Comment: Plantar and dorsal interosseous muscles form the fourth muscle layer of the foot. Similar to the interossei of the hand, the dorsal interossei abduct the digits (toes) and extend the distal phalanges.

Clinical: One can test the dorsal interosseous muscles by asking the patient to spread the toes against resistance.
