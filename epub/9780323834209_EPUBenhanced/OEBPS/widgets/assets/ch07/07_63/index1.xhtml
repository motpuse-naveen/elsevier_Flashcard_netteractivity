Nerves of Gluteal Region and Thigh: Posterior View

Comment: Nerves to the gluteal region and posterior thigh arise from the sacral plexus. They come largely from the anterior rami of L4-S4.

The major nerves of the gluteal region are the superior and inferior gluteal nerves. The largest nerve of the sacral plexus is the sciatic nerve, made up of anterior rami of L4-S3. The sciatic nerve innervates all muscles of the posterior compartment of the thigh. Through its two terminal branches, the tibial and common fibular nerves, it also innervates all muscles below the knee.

Clinical: Intramuscular gluteal injections are given in the upper outer quadrant of the buttock to avoid injuring the sciatic nerve, which passes just inferior to or through the piriformis muscle in the middle of the gluteal region.

Pulled or torn muscle injuries to the hamstring muscles are common sports injuries often caused by quick starts, as in sprint races, or from kicking a ball. If torn, it is not uncommon for the muscle to tear from its proximal attachment to the ischial tuberosity.
