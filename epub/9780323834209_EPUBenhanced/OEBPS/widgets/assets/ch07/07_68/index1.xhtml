Comment: Dermatomes (the unilateral area of skin that is innervated by the general sensory fibers of a single spinal nerve) generally are based upon the segmental embryonic somite development. However, there usually is overlap at a specific dermatome site on the skin by sensory nerve fibers one level cranial and one level caudal to the specific dermatome site. Dermatomes on the trunk are fairly linear horizontally, but on the limbs they spiral due to limb rotation during fetal development. Hence, limb dermatome maps typically are approximations. The upper limb rotation is fairly uniform (see Cards 6-61 and 6-62), but the lower limb rotation results in a spiral pattern arrangement, leading to several “autonomous sensory zones” shown in Cards 7-66 and 7-67. However, a clinical evidence-based dermatome map of the upper and lower limb (Lee et al., Clin Anat 21[5]:363;2008) demonstrates that there are some relatively uniform “autonomous” sensory zones, as shown on this card of the lower limb. In light of this study, clinicians must be aware that the “traditional” dermatome maps, especially of the lower limb, may need to be revised. However, variability can still exist among patients.
